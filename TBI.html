Understanding Traumatic Brain Injuries (TBI) and Concussions

Traumatic Brain Injuries (TBI) are a broad category of brain injuries that can result from significant impacts, such as those sustained in car accidents or slip and fall incidents. Concussions are a form of mild TBI, typically caused by a blow or jolt to the head that disrupts normal brain function. Both TBIs and concussions can lead to a variety of cognitive, physical, and emotional symptoms.

Importance of Early Diagnosis

Early and accurate diagnosis of TBIs and concussions is crucial for effective treatment and recovery. Prompt intervention can help manage symptoms and prevent long-term complications.

Comprehensive TBI and Concussion Testing

Learn More

At MyPhoneTherapies, our team of experienced neuropsychologists offers thorough testing to accurately diagnose the severity and impact of your injury. Our testing includes:

  • Detailed History: Gathering information about the incident, symptoms, and any previous head injuries or concussions.
  • Neuropsychological Evaluation: Assessing cognitive functions such as memory, attention, executive functioning, and processing speed.
  • Standardized Assessments: Utilizing evidence-based tools and questionnaires to evaluate cognitive and emotional impacts.

Personalized Therapy Plans

Learn More

Based on the results of your testing, our neuropsychologists create customized therapy plans tailored to your specific needs. Our approach includes:

  • Cognitive Rehabilitation: Exercises and activities designed to improve memory, attention, and problem-solving skills.
  • TBI and Concussion-Specific Counseling: Addressing the emotional and psychological symptoms related to these injuries, such as headache management, dizziness and balance, memory and concentration, mood changes, sleep issues.
  • Regular Monitoring: Continuous assessment of your progress and adjustment of your therapy plan as needed.
