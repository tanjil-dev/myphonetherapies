Understanding PTSD After an Accident

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is a serious mental health condition that can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event, such as a car accident or slip and fall. PTSD can cause a range of symptoms, including flashbacks, severe anxiety, and uncontrollable thoughts about the event.

Why PTSD Occurs:

Learn More

After an accident, the trauma of the event can leave lasting emotional and psychological effects. Factors contributing to PTSD after an accident include:

  • Life-Threatening Experiences During the Accident: The fear and danger felt during the accident.
  • Physical Injuries: Chronic pain and physical limitations can remind individuals of the traumatic auto accident or premise liability accident.
  • Emotional Shock: The sudden and unexpected nature of the accident.
  • Recurring Thoughts: Inability to stop thinking about the accident.
